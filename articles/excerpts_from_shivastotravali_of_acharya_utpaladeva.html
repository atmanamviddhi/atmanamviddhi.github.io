Excerpts from Shivastotravali of Acharya Utpaladeva

I bow to the lustrous devotee,
Who without meditation, or muttering phrases
Spontaneously just like that,
Realized the auspicious self. — Shiva

chapter: 1 verse: 1

For a devotee bestowed with prosperity of your grace,
Living in your city - universal consciousness
O Lord! Even the worldly journey
Is the same blissful exhilaration.

chapter: 1 verse: 3

O Lord! For your devotees,
What place is not sacred?
What incantation unsuccessful?
In all these worlds, verily your identity.

chapter: 1 verse: 4

Having destroyed all obstructions, (of ignorance)
Through the splendour of enveloping effulgent devotion,
And realized the true nature of everything, (I request)
Even traces of anxiety to vanish from me.

chapter: 1 verse: 9

What a wonder, O Lord,
Mind the seed of only misery, by nature
Bears magnificent fruit, blissful tranquillity,
When drenched in the nectar, "your devotion".

chapter: 1 verse: 26

Obeisance to Thee! The unique benedictive (Shiva) fire,
Dreadful, as cause to burn (duality of) the worlds,
Cool and calm, destroying all woe and worry,
Tender alluring, super ambrocia—bliss of awareness.

chapter: 2 verse: 3

Obeisance to Thee! O God of gods
The unique identity of “I” conscience
The Supreme Lord, destroyer of duality
Bestower of benediction, tranquillity incarnate.

chapter: 2 verse: 4

Obeisance to Thee! The benedictive fire
Terribly gruesome, accepting as an oblation
The molten marrow of all the three worlds, cut
Monom to pieces (dualism)
But still auspiciousness personified.

chapter: 2 verse: 5

Obeisance to Him! The illuminant Lord, so
Wonderful unique benefactor,
Whose greatest qualification is,
Being unconnected with all
connected (unconcerned) with qualifications.

chapter: 2 verse: 6

Obeisance to Thee! The abstruse Lord!
The opponent of Vedas and Tantras,
The creator of Vedas and Tantras,
And the true basis of Vedas and Tantras

chapter: 2 verse: 7

Obeisance to Thee! The benedictive Shiva
The one cause of the worlds, (creator)
The only enemy of the worlds, (destroyer)
The world-shaped one, and one beyond (above) it.

chapter: 2 verse: 8

True, untrue (Existing non-existing)
All objects are rightly catalogued in these two,
There is a third beyond them,
Obeisance, to that wonderment, Lord Shiva

chapter: 3 verse: 1

Surely, untruth is different from truth.
And truth differs from untruth.
My Lord, you are beyond these two
Are therefore personification of both truth and untruth.

chapter: 3 verse: 18

O Lord! Till I become instantaneously,
Your fully aware graceful servant,
Kindly manifest your avenues to me,
And hide all worldly demeanour, from me.

chapter: 4 verse: 3

O Lord! when shall I start,
Dancing in exultation (oneness with you),
With hair fully dyed (graced)
In heaps of dust, from your lotus feet(benevolence).

chapter: 5 verse: 2

At times; O Lord! Something so great happens sometimes (in samadhi)
In the hearts (of devotees) touched (blessed) by the bottom of your feet,
When all this world, desolves in a nectarian ocean of self conscient pleasure.
Kindly give me that bliss forever.

chapter: 5 verse: 26

O Lord ! He who realizes all this, changeful world,
As your changeless identity
How on this earth, can fear come to,
That, ever happy, self conscient perfect person.

chapter: 13 verse: 16

In between piety and sin, between two actions,
Two perceptions, and, inbetween the pairs of joy etc.
Your devotees, My Lord ! Enjoy (realize)
Wonderful, blissful happiness. How great,
My Lord.
